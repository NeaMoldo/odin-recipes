Sarmale in pickled cabbage leaves

Description

Sarmale are the soul of Romanian cuisine, cherished at every celebration. These savory rolls consist of a flavorful mixture of minced meat (usually pork or beef), rice, and traditional spices, snugly wrapped in fermented cabbage leaves or delicate grape leaves. Slow-cooked for hours in a clay pot with tomato juice and smoked meat, they achieve a tender, melt-in-your-mouth texture. Best served hot with mămăligă, a dollop of sour cream, and a spicy chili pepper.

Ingredients

  • 1 large pickled cabbage (sour cabbage/sauerkraut head)
  • 1 kg ground pork (minced pork)
  • 500 g smoked pork (preferably ribs or pork rind/cracklings)
  • 200 g smoked bacon (slab bacon or fatback
  • 1 cup of rice
  • 2 large onions
  • 2 cups of white wine
  • 1 glass of tomato broth (tomato juice/puree)
  • 3 canned tomatoes (in tomato juice)
  • Seasonings: Thyme, dill, bay leaves, paprika, pepper, and salt

Steps

  1. Carefully unwrap the pickled cabbage leaves and rinse them thoroughly to remove excess salt. If the leaves are too large, cut them in half. Be sure to remove the thick ribs (the hard central veins) to make the leaves more pliable and easier to roll. Finely chop any remaining cabbage and set it aside; you will use this for layering the bottom and top of the pot.
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the finely chopped onions in a little oil or lard until they turn golden brown. Add the rinsed rice and continue to sauté for a few minutes until the grains become translucent. In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground pork, the finely chopped smoked meat, and the smoked bacon. Add the sautéed onion and rice mixture, then season with salt, pepper, thyme, and chopped dill. You can also add sweet paprika if you like. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly to achieve a smooth, well-blended consistency.
  3. Take a cabbage leaf and place a generous tablespoon of filling at one end. Roll the leaf tightly, starting from the end with the filling, and tuck the side edges inward to seal the roll. Repeat this process until you have used all of the meat mixture.
  4. Grease the bottom of a large clay or cast-iron pot with lard or oil. Spread a layer of the finely chopped cabbage across the bottom, followed by a layer of smoked ribs or pork rinds. Arrange the sarmale in layers, alternating with chopped cabbage and pieces of smoked meat. The final top layer should be chopped cabbage, sprinkled with bay leaves and sprigs of thyme.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix the white wine with the tomato broth and warm the mixture slightly. Pour it over the sarmale, ensuring the liquid covers them completely. Cover the pot and place it over low heat. Simmer the sarmale gently for at least 5 hours until the cabbage becomes very tender and the flavors are perfectly infused. If the liquid reduces too much during this time, you can add a little more hot water.
  6. Sarmale are best served piping hot, accompanied by a dollop of sour cream, a spicy chili pepper, and warm mămăligă. They are also famously even tastier the next day, after they have rested and the flavors have had more time to meld together perfectly. This is the classic recipe for sarmale with pickled cabbage—a traditional dish that offers a true feast for the senses at every meal.
